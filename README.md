## What KYC on OKX Actually Means

KYC stands for "Know Your Customer." It's the regulatory and security process financial platforms use to confirm you are who you say you are. For OKX, KYC isn't optional decoration — it's a legal requirement tied to anti-money laundering (AML) laws, counter-terrorism financing rules, and the local regulations of every jurisdiction where OKX operates.

Here's what KYC does on OKX in practical terms:

- **Confirms your identity** through a government-issued photo ID and a live selfie
- **Complies with global financial regulations**, including GDPR in the EU and SSN-based reporting in the U.S.
- **Protects your account** against takeover, fraud, and unauthorized withdrawals
- **Unlocks platform features** — deposit, spot trading, futures, staking, NFT purchases, and withdrawals are all gated behind verification

You can browse markets, view prices, and read OKX's learning resources without KYC. But the moment you want to deposit, trade, or move crypto out, OKX requires at least basic identity verification. This is the same standard you'd hit at any regulated exchange — Coinbase, Kraken, Binance all do versions of the same thing — but OKX's tiered structure means the level you complete directly determines how much you can withdraw and which features you can touch.

> One thing worth knowing upfront: KYC on OKX isn't a one-time event. OKX describes it as a "continuous process." You may be asked to re-verify when your ID expires, when regulations in your region change, or when your account activity shifts significantly. Enhanced Due Diligence (EDD) can also trigger requests for proof of address or source of funds on high-volume accounts.

## Documents You Need for OKX KYC Verification

Before you start the verification flow, gather the following. OKX is strict about document quality — blurry uploads and mismatches are the number-one cause of rejections.

**Core documents (all regions):**

- **Government-issued photo ID** — passport, national ID card, or driver's license. Must be valid, unexpired, and show your full name, date of birth, issue date, and expiration date.
- **Live selfie** — taken in real time through the OKX app or webcam. Your full face must fit inside the oval frame, no masks, no hats, no glare from the camera flash.
- **Proof of address** (required for Level 2 and above) — utility bill, bank statement, or government correspondence. Must be dated within the last 3 months for most regions, or within the last 6 months for EEA users. Your name and address on the document must match your ID exactly.

**U.S.-specific additions:**

- **Social Security Number (SSN)** — required for U.S. residents as part of regulatory compliance
- Driver's license or state ID is typically the preferred ID type for U.S. users

**Document quality rules OKX enforces:**

- No screenshots of any kind — original photos or scans only
- All four corners of the document must be visible
- Text must be sharp and legible, no blur, no glare, no reflections
- The document cannot be expired
- Information on the ID must match your registration details exactly

> Pro tip: take your ID photos in natural light against a dark, plain background. Hold the document flat. Don't photograph it through a plastic sleeve. These sound obvious, but they're the difference between a 30-second approval and a 24-hour review.

## Common KYC Rejection Reasons and How to Fix Them

Most rejections fall into a small set of categories. Here's what goes wrong and how to correct it:

| Rejection Reason | What Happened | How to Fix |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Blurry or unclear ID photo** | Image was out of focus, low resolution, or had glare | Retake in natural light against a dark background; hold the document flat; clean the camera lens first |
| **Expired document** | The ID you uploaded has passed its expiration date | Use a different, valid ID. If you don't have one, renew your ID before retrying |
| **Information mismatch** | Name, birthdate, or address on the ID doesn't match what you entered in the form | Re-enter your basic information to match the ID exactly — same spelling, same order, same date format |
| **Cropped document** | One or more corners of the ID are cut off | Retake the photo with the entire document visible, including all four corners and any holographic edges |
| **Wrong document type** | You uploaded something OKX doesn't accept for your region | Check OKX's regional requirements — U.S. users need a driver's license or state ID; EU users typically use passport or national ID |
| **Proof of address too old** | The utility bill or bank statement is older than the allowed window | Get a more recent document — within 3 months for most regions, 6 months for EEA |
| **Name mismatch on proof of address** | The name on your utility bill doesn't match your ID | Use a document in your own name, or contact OKX support if you can't obtain one |

If you've been rejected more than twice, stop resubmitting and contact OKX Support through the in-app live chat or the help center ticket system. Include your account details, the rejection reason, and a clear description of what you've already tried. The support team can often see something the automated system missed.

## Why Your OKX Withdrawal Is Blocked (and How to Fix It)

This is one of the most-searched KYC-related problems on OKX, and it almost always traces back to one of four causes:

**1. Incomplete or failed KYC**

If your verification is incomplete, expired, or was rejected, OKX restricts withdrawals until you fix it. The fix: go to **Profile > Identity Verification**, check your current status, and complete or resubmit the missing level.

**2. Exceeded withdrawal limit for your KYC tier**

Each tier has a hard daily cap. If you try to withdraw more than your tier allows, the transaction is blocked automatically. The fix: either wait until the next 24-hour window, or upgrade to a higher KYC tier by submitting the additional documents (proof of address for Level 2, business docs for Level 3).

**3. Unusual account activity or security flags**

Large or rapid withdrawals, withdrawals to a new address, or activity that deviates from your usual pattern can trigger a security hold. These holds are a fraud-prevention measure. The fix: wait for the review to complete (usually 24 to 48 hours), or contact support with your account details and withdrawal ID to expedite.

**4. Recent account changes**

Changing your password, device, or security settings can temporarily lock withdrawals as a precaution against unauthorized access. The lock usually clears on its own within 24 to 72 hours.

> If your account is frozen entirely — not just withdrawals blocked, but the account itself — OKX typically gives a grace period during which you can still withdraw funds even while full account access is suspended. Complete the verification within that window and full functionality is restored.
